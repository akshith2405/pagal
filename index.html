The Scroll

Honestly, there’s no right time to meet somebody new and maybe that’s why I keep delaying the inevitability of me finding you, the real you. The though of the rel you makes me quiver. The opportunity might be out there, but getting lost just isn’t worth the time as I’m not a teenager anymore, and not willing to convince somebody to be mine. You’d shake your head that I know that you wouldn’t need convincing, but solace to see what my retinas see, but that doesn’t change the fact in the past, my heart has been left wincing does that hurt to hear? I guess I do wonder how we’ll meet again, Will it be in a sunny restaurant, or will you come to me in the street or maybe I’ll bump into you while you take your sutta walk to vihaan. I have a bad habit of being in my head. Maybe I’ll drop some coffee drop on my apron when I see you or drop my favorite loaf of sourdough bread. Would you smile and heeheehee, when I turn red, the embarrassment flushes my cheeks or would you walk away from it hesitantly and think about my laugh for weeks. Would you stay a little longer when our hands raise for just a second or would you pull back wishing it had been for much much more, cause I would sweep you up off the floor. Like I said, there’s no right time to meet someone new but I could never explain someone like you.
